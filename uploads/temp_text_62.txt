PMG201c - Practical Exam 1 (Fall 2025)
You have developed a unique, niche consumer product, and your project is to validate its market viability through a temporary pop-up retail operation. The project requires securing permits, designing and building the display, managing inventory, training student sales staff, and achieving a sales goal of $15,000 in gross revenue. The pop-up operation must run for two consecutive weeks. The setup and operational costs (excluding product manufacturing cost) are strictly limited to $1500, and the budget constraint demands that the total inventory shrinkage/loss be kept below 2% of the total inventory value. For quality, the customer satisfaction rating, measured by post-purchase surveys, must exceed 90% positive feedback regarding product quality and the retail experience.
Request 1 (20%): develop a narrative charter statement that includes the following details
1. Project name,
2. Justifications (purpose & reasons to implement this project),
3. Project constraints, in terms of scope, time, cost/budget, and quality.
Request 2 (20%): provide at least five main cost/budget items. Each cost/budget item, you need to provide following details: name, description, estimation along with the way to estimate (how to estimate, person in charged,..)
Request 3 (30%): develop communication plan for the project where you:
1. Define at three project stakeholders (project-internal, organization-internal, external)
2. Create a communication plan for each of those stakeholders and the communications you believe they should receive. Each communication needs to include following details: information, purpose, frequency, method or format, responsible.
Request 4 (30%): identify three project risks by listing out the risk title/name, description, possible impacts (in term of scope or quality, time, and cost), and relevant risk response plans (mitigation, contingency).
Request 1
1. Project Name
Niche Consumer Product Market Validation via Pop-up Retail
2. Project Justifications (Purpose & Reasons)
The purpose of this project is to validate the market viability of a unique, niche consumer product through a real-world selling environment.
Instead of relying solely on surveys or online testing, the project uses a temporary pop-up retail operation to directly observe customer behavior, purchasing decisions, and satisfaction.
This project is implemented to:
Test real customer demand and willingness to pay for the product.
Collect direct feedback on product quality and retail experience.
Evaluate operational feasibility, including inventory management and sales staff performance.
Reduce long-term business risk before committing to large-scale production or permanent retail investment.
3. Project Constraints
a. Scope Constraints
The project scope is limited to planning, setting up, and operating one temporary pop-up retail store.
Included activities: securing permits, designing and building the product display, managing inventory, training student sales staff, conducting sales, and collecting customer feedback.
Excluded from scope: product manufacturing, long-term retail operations, online sales platforms, and post-project marketing campaigns beyond feedback analysis.
b. Time Constraints
The pop-up retail operation must run for two consecutive weeks only.
All preparation activities, including permits, display setup, and staff training, must be completed before the opening date.
The project must conclude immediately after the two-week operation and post-sale evaluation.
c. Cost / Budget Constraints
Total setup and operational costs (excluding product manufacturing costs) are strictly capped at USD 1,500.
All expenses related to permits, display materials, staffing support, and operations must remain within this fixed budget.
No budget overrun is allowed.
d. Quality Constraints
Inventory shrinkage or loss must be kept below 2% of total inventory value.
Customer satisfaction, measured through post-purchase surveys, must achieve more than 90% positive feedback related to product quality and retail experience.
Sales staff must be adequately trained to ensure consistent service quality throughout the operation.

Request 4
Risk 1: Low Sales Performance
Risk Description:
Sales revenue falls below the USD 15,000 target due to low customer traffic or insufficient product attractiveness.
Possible Impacts:
Scope / Quality: Failure to validate market feasibility
Time: Inability to recover lost sales due to the fixed two-week pop-up duration
Cost: Operational expenses cannot be recovered through sales revenue
Probability: Medium (3)
Impact: High (4)
Risk Score: 3 × 4 = 12 (High)
Mitigation Plan:
Select a high-traffic pop-up retail location
Train sales staff in product knowledge and consultative selling techniques
Introduce promotional campaigns and bundled product offers
Contingency Plan:
Apply short-term price discounts if sales performance remains low
Intensify selling activities during peak customer hours
Focus sales efforts on best-selling products
Risk 2: Inventory Loss / Shrinkage Exceeding 2%
Risk Description:
Inventory loss caused by theft, damage, or mishandling results in shrinkage exceeding the permitted 2% limit.
Possible Impacts:
Scope / Quality: Inventory control and quality requirements are not met
Time: Additional time required for inventory reconciliation and investigation
Cost: Increased operational costs and reduced profitability
Probability: Medium (3)
Impact: High (4)
Risk Score: 3 × 4 = 12 (High)
Mitigation Plan:
Perform daily inventory counts and reconciliation
Clearly assign inventory management responsibilities
Restrict physical access to storage and inventory areas
Contingency Plan:
Temporarily suspend the sale of high-risk inventory items
Adjust pricing strategies to partially offset inventory losses
Increase monitoring and supervision at sales counters
Risk 3: Low Customer Satisfaction Rating
Risk Description:
Customer dissatisfaction with product quality or the retail experience leads to positive feedback falling below the 90% threshold.
Possible Impacts:
Scope / Quality: Project quality objectives are not achieved
Time: Additional effort and time required to resolve customer complaints
Cost: Extra costs associated with refunds, exchanges, or customer incentives
Probability: Low to Medium (2)
Impact: Major (4)
Risk Score: 2 × 4 = 8 (Medium)
Mitigation Plan:
Provide customer service training to all sales staff
Conduct quality inspections on products prior to sale
Design a clear, intuitive, and customer-friendly store layout
Contingency Plan:
Resolve customer complaints immediately at the point of sale
Offer product exchanges, refunds, or goodwill incentives
Adjust sales and service processes based on customer feedback
